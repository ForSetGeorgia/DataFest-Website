Alberto Cairo
Knight Chair in Visual Journalism @ School of Communication of the University of Miami | USA
Alberto Cairo is the Knight Chair in Visual Journalism at the School of Communication of the University of Miami (UM). He is also the director of the visualization program at UM’s Institute for Data Science and Computing. He leads graphics teams at news publications in Spain and Brazil. He is the author of 'How Charts Lie: Getting Smarter About Visual Information' (2019), 'The Truthful Art' (2016) and 'The Functional Art’ (2013). Cairo currently consults with companies like Google, and has provided visualization training to the European Union, Eurostat, the CDC, the Army National Guard, and many others. He lives in Miami, Florida.
Yaryna Serkez
Graphics Editor @ New York Times | Germany
Yaryna Serkez is a full-stack data journalist. She is Graphics Editor on Opinion at the New York Times. Previously she worked for The Wall Street Journal where she created interactive data stories. Her skill set includes a sharp eye for design and an ability to create web-based 3D explorations. She is based in Germany.
Harry Stevens
Visual Journalist @ Washington Post | USA
Harry is a visual journalist at the Washington Post, where he creates news graphics for both the web and print. Prior to joining the Post, he worked for Axios, The Hindustan Times, and the Salt Lake Tribune. He has a Master’s degree in journalism from Columbia University.
Nadieh Bremer
Data Visualization Designer @ Visual Cinnamon | Netherlands
Nadieh Bremer is a graduated Astronomer, turned data scientist, turned data visualization designer, based near Amsterdam. She's working as a freelancer under the name "Visual Cinnamon". As 2017's "Best Individual" in the Information is Beautiful Awards, she focuses on uniquely crafted (interactive) data visualizations that both engage and enlighten its audience. Working for companies such as Google News Lab & UNESCO to small start-ups. From printed magazines to interactive experiences online to more promotional focused visuals for press releases, data-driven reports, and data art.
Gabrielle Merite
Independent Data Visualization Designer | USA
Gabrielle Merite is an independent French Data Visualization Designer. Former scientific journalist specialized in Health and Biology, she turned to design in order to merge her love for science & art. Her work focuses on illuminating the stories behind data. She has collaborated with a variety of international leaders from government agencies such as the French Agency for Research on AIDS and Viral Hepatitis, the United Nations to independent companies like ViiV HealthCare and Cleo Capital.
Sonja Kuijpers
Founder @ STUDIO TERP | Netherlands
Sonja Kuijpers runs STUDIO TERP, her one-woman information graphics studio. In 2013 she founded her studio and started creating infographics and data visualisations for clients. With a former career in public space / landscape architecture design, she has a fundamental experience in analysis and its visualization. She also designs her own independent dataviz and dataart projects and received an Information is Beautiful Gold award for her personal project “A view on despair”. Creating data visualisation to Sonja, is trying to locate herself in the data, making sense of numbers with a human approach, showing insights as well as the aesthetics of information and data
Ajit Jaoker
Course Director @ University of Oxford | UK
Ajit's work spans research, entrepreneurship and academia relating to artificial intelligence (AI) and the internet of things (IoT). He is the course director of the course: Artificial Intelligence: Cloud and Edge Implementations.at the University of Oxford. Besides this, he also conducts the University of Oxford courses: AI for Cybersecurity and Computer Vision. Ajit works as a Data Scientist through his company feynlabs - focusing on building innovative early stage AI prototypes for domains such as cybersecurity, robotics and healthcare. He is also currently working on a book to teach AI using mathematical foundations at high school level.
Stephen Redmond
Studio Lead @ Accenture | Ireland
Based at The Dock, Accenture’s Global Innovation Centre in Dbulin, Stephen’s team delivers data innovation solutions for Accenture. He has extensive experience with AI and Machine Learning, Big Data, Graph Databases, Digital Assistants, Advanced Analytics, Data Architecture and occasionally some Data Visualisation. With over twenty-five years’ experience delivering data and analytics projects both in the public and private sectors, he frequently presents on data topics to a mix of business and technical audiences. Stephen is a published author and has a global reputation, including being recognised as a Qlik Luminary (2014 through 2020).
Amanda Makulec
Senior Data Visualization Lead @ Excella | USA
Amanda Makulec is the Senior Data Visualization Lead at Excella and holds a Masters of Public Health from the Boston University School of Public Health. She worked with data in global health programs for eight years before joining Excella, where she leads teams and develops user-centered data visualization products for federal, non-profit, and private sector clients. Amanda volunteers as the Operations Director for the Data Visualization Society and is a co-organizer for Data Visualization DC.
Neil Richards
Data Visualization Consultant & Evangelist @ Groupon | UK
Neil Richards is a 2020 Tableau Zen Master and Public Ambassador, and works as a Data Visualization Consultant and Evangelist at Groupon in the UK where he is responsible for internal community building, data viz education and visualisation best practices within the organisation. He is also Knowledge Director of the Data Visualization Society and an active Board Member for Viz for Social Good. Neil also blogs at questionsindataviz.com where he regularly discusses and demonstrates his personal (and often unorthodox) data visualisation projects.
Valentina D'Efilippo
Designer & Creative Director | UK
Named one of Campaign’s 30 Future Creative Female Leaders, Valentina D'Efilippo is an award-winning designer, creative director, and co-author of The Infographic History of the World. Her work takes many forms – from theatre productions and exhibitions to editorial content and digital experiences. She also leads a series of Masterclasses with The Guardian.
Paul Radu
Director @ Organized Crime and Corruption Reporting Project | Romania
Paul Radu is a director and co-founder of the Organized Crime and Corruption Reporting Project, a co-creator of the Investigative Dashboard concept, of Visual Investigative Scenarios visualization software and a co-founder of RISE Project - a platform for investigative reporters and hackers in Romania. He is the recipient of numerous awards including the Knight International Journalism Award, the Investigative Reporters and Editors Award, the Global Shining Light Award, the Tom Renner Investigative Reporters and Editors Award, the Daniel Pearl Award for Outstanding International Investigative Reporting and the European Press Prize. Paul is an Ashoka Global fellow - innovating investigative journalism and its coverage of transnational organized crime; and a board member with the Global Investigative Journalism Network.
Han Xiao
Founder & CEO @ Jina AI | Germany
Han Xiao is Founder & CEO of Jina AI Ltd. He is also an AI open-source evangelist. His Fashion-MNIST and bert-as-service were listed as the most popular AI open-source projects in 2017&18 world-widely. Previously, Han led a team on neural information retrieval at Tencent AI, laying down the next-gen search infrastructure. He served in the Tencent Technical Advisory Council and Opensource Program Office, fostering the open-source and DevOps culture inside the company. Han also served as a board member at LF AI Foundation, driving the open source innovation in AI by enabling international collaboration. Han received the Ph.D. and M.Sc. in computer science from the Technical University of Munich in Germany. He is also the Founder & Chairman of the German-Chinese Association of AI, a NPO in Germany.
Federica Fragapane
Independent Information Designer | Italy
Federica Fragapane is an independent information designer. Among her projects, she designed data visualizations for Google News Lab, UN, Scientific American, BBC Science Focus, Penguin Books and La Lettura. She is co-author of the infographics children's book “Planet Earth”, the "Geopolitical Atlas of Water" and author of “The Stories Behind a Line” a visual narrative of six asylum seekers’ journeys. Many of her projects have an experimental approach and she is interested in exploring the relationship between data visualization and people.
Lasha Antadze
Founder @ Shelf.Network | Georgia
Lasha Antadze is a founder of Shelf.Network - a technology company facilitating cross-border vehicle trades by enabling connectivity among the value chain participants, and building global infrastructure in partnership with: BNP Paribas, Arval, Carfax, IAAI, Adesa etc. Currently the company is active with US to Europe and Japan to South East Asia networks. Lasha has experience in distributed system architecting and data management for P2P networks.
Solomiya Branets
Data Scientist @ Monese | Estonia
Solomiya Brantes is a Data Scientist at Monese, UK-based digital bank, for which she builds machine learning models that detect fraud and money laundering. She is based in Estonia where she obtained her Master’s degree in Quantitative Economics from the University of Tartu. Her master's research innovatively applied data science for anti-money laundering and has been awarded a prize by Estonian Research Council and one of the biggest Estonian banks.
